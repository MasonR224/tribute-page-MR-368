Early Career & Rise to Fame

  • 2006 Started playing NES Tetris competitively and became known for his exceptional skills.
  • 2010 Won the inaugural Classic Tetris World Championship (CTWC)establishing himself as the best Classic Tetris player.

CTWC Dominance

  • 2010-2013 Won four consecutive CTWC titles.
  • 2015-2017 Won three more CTWC titles, bringing his total to seven championships.
  • 2017 Achieved the first-ever "Maxout" (999,999 points) on Level 31 in live competition.
  • 2017 Featured in the Tetris documentary Ecstasy of Order: The Tetris Masters.

Legacy & Achievements

  • 2018 Competed in the CTWC finals but was defeated by Joseph Saelee in a legendary match.
  • 2018 Achieved the first-ever documented Level 33 in NES Tetris.
  • 2019 Continued competing and streaming, mentoring younger players.
  • 2020 Became a well-known Twitch streamer, inspiring a new generation of Tetris players.

Passing & Tributes

  • January 5, 2021 Jonas Neubauer passed away at age 39 due to a sudden medical emergency.
  • 2021 The Tetris community and CTWC honored his legacy, with tributes from players worldwide.
  • 2021 The CTWC introduced the Jonas Neubauer Memorial Award, given to players who embody sportsmanship and passion for Tetris.
  • 2022-Present His influence continues to be seen in the growing competitive Tetris scene, with many players citing him as their inspiration.
